VIRUSES

Definition: Tiny infectious agents that cannot survive or reproduce on their own; they need a host cell. • Size: Usually 20–300 nanometers — way smaller than bacteria. • Structure: • Genetic material (DNA or RNA) • Protein coat (capsid) • Sometimes an outer lipid envelope How they work: 1. Virus attaches to a host cell. 2. Injects its genetic material. 3. Hijacks the cell’s machinery to make new viruses. 4. Cell often dies or bursts, releasing new viruses.
